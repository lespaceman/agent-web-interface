# AthenaFlow Rename — Brainstorm & Analysis

## The Vision (Pivoted)

~~Workflow runtime for AI coding agents.~~

**AthenaFlow is the universal interface provider for AI agents.**

Agents are powerful — but they need a way to interact with the world. AthenaFlow provides the interface layer connecting agents to **any surface**: web browsers, mobile apps, desktop applications, TVs, iOS devices, custom industrial hardware, machines, and people.

**Core positioning**: *"Workflows are only possible with the right tools. We provide the tools."*

Think: **Stripe for payments** → universal interface to financial systems.
AthenaFlow → universal interface from agents to the physical and digital world.

## Recommended Names

### Tier 1 — Top Picks (Zero collision in tech/dev/AI)

#### 1. Kontrol

**Stylized "control" — agents take control of any surface**

- Syllables: 2 (kon-TROL)
- Domains to target: `kontrol.dev`, `kontrol.sh`, `kontrol.so`, `kontrol.run`
- Why it works: Directly says what the product does — gives agents **control** over web, mobile, desktop, TV, hardware. The K-spelling makes it ownable and trademark-distinct. Strong Germanic/Nordic feel signals precision engineering. Works in every context: "Kontrol connects your agent to any device." CLI: `kontrol connect`, `kontrol bridge`.
- Tone: Precise, powerful, enterprise-grade
- Collision check: **kontrol.tech** exists (Austrian autonomous vehicle compliance, different space). No collision in dev tools / AI agent space.
- Marketing potential: *"Total Kontrol."* / *"Give your agents Kontrol."*

---

#### 2. Pipewright

**"Pipe" (pipeline/channel) + "Wright" (craftsman/builder)**

- Syllables: 2 (PIPE-right)
- Domains to target: `pipewright.dev`, `pipewright.io`
- Why it works: Follows the **Playwright** naming pattern — developers already associate "-wright" with reliable, well-crafted tooling. "Pipe" means channel/connection, not just CI pipeline. Conveys: *"We are master craftsmen of the pipes that connect agents to the world."* Still works post-pivot because "pipe" = connection channel, not just workflow.
- Tone: Professional, developer-native, premium
- Collision check: **Clean** — no software products found
- Marketing potential: *"Expertly crafted interfaces."* / *"Your agents, plumbed into everything."*

#### 3. Interfold

**"Interface" + "Manifold" — many interfaces, folded into one**

- Syllables: 3 (IN-ter-fold)
- Domains to target: `interfold.dev`, `interfold.io`
- Why it works: Perfectly captures the product — folding web, mobile, desktop, TV, industrial hardware interfaces into a single unified layer. "Inter" = between. "Fold" = bring together. Novel compound word with immediate meaning. No pronunciation ambiguity.
- Tone: Technical, elegant, sophisticated
- Collision check: **Clean** — no software products found
- Marketing potential: *"Every interface, one fold."* / *"Interfold: the universal surface layer for agents."*

#### 4. Pantouch

**"Pan" (all, everything) + "Touch" (interact, reach)**

- Domains to target: `pantouch.dev`, `pantouch.io`

- Tone: Clean, modern, approachable yet professional

## Top Pick: **Kontrol**

**Why Kontrol wins for this vision:**

1. **Universality**: "Control" is the most direct word for what agents need — control over devices, surfaces, systems. It's not limited to web, or mobile, or hardware. It's everything.
2. **The K**: Makes it trademark-ownable, visually distinctive, and impossible to confuse with the English word in search results. Germanic/Nordic precision connotation.
3. **Enterprise appeal**: CISOs, CTOs, and engineering VPs respond to "control." It implies governance, reliability, security.
4. **Scale of ambition**: "Kontrol" doesn't box you into any single interface type. Web today, industrial robots tomorrow. The name grows with the product.
5. **CLI elegance**: `kontrol connect web`, `kontrol connect mobile`, `kontrol connect hardware`
6. **Domain strategy**: kontrol.dev is the natural home. kontrol.sh for the CLI. kontrol.run for the docs.

**Runner-up: Interfold** — if you want a name that more explicitly describes the multi-interface unification aspect. It's more descriptive, less abstract, and completely collision-free.

## Domain Strategy Notes

For any chosen name, the recommended approach:
- **Primary**: `.dev` (Google-managed, HTTPS-enforced, developer credibility)
- **CLI/package**: `.sh` (shell connotation, great for dev tools)
- **Docs**: `.run` or `.io`
- **Defensive**: Register the `.com` variant if available, even as a redirect

**Important**: Actual domain availability must be verified on a registrar (Namecheap, Porkbun, Cloudflare). This analysis covers brand/product collisions, not DNS-level domain registration status.
